# Perplexity Computer Prompt: Site Analysis Carousel

## Copy this prompt into Perplexity Computer:

---

Create a 10-slide Instagram carousel (1080x1350px portrait) for @studwalldan - a female architecture educator in Dubai who runs a paid Skool community ($29/mo) for architecture students.

TOPIC: "How to do a site analysis that actually impresses your lecturers"

CONTENT FOR EACH SLIDE:

Slide 1 (HOOK): Bold text: "Your site analysis is probably just a sun path and some wind arrows." Smaller text below: "Here's what's actually missing." Background: clean white/off-white. No images needed - text-only hook slide.

Slide 2 (THE PROBLEM): "Most students collect data. Top students analyze it." Show a simple comparison: LEFT side labeled "What you're doing" with icons for sun path, wind rose, site photos. RIGHT side labeled "What lecturers actually want" with icons for design implications, site-driven principles, analysis diagrams. Use a simple dividing line between the two.

Slide 3 (STEP 1 - RESEARCH): "Step 1: Research before you visit." Bullet list: Google Street View (toggle historical years), Zoning + height limits, Surrounding context within 500m, Precedent projects on similar sites. Include a simple SVG mockup of a Google Street View interface or aerial map.

Slide 4 (STEP 2 - ON SITE): "Step 2: What to actually document on-site." Bullet list: First impressions (3 words that describe how the site FEELS), Movement patterns (where do people walk, stop, avoid?), Sound level (rate 1-5), Views FROM and OF the site, Edge conditions. Include a simple plan diagram with arrows showing movement patterns.

Slide 5 (THE KEY FRAMEWORK): "The one thing that separates good from great site analysis." Big text: "For every piece of data, complete this sentence:" Then show: "[DATA] means that [DESIGN IMPLICATION]" Example: "Prevailing SW wind" → "Living spaces face NE, or use wind for natural ventilation." This is the most important slide - make it visually clear and memorable.

Slide 6 (MORE EXAMPLES): "More examples of data → design implications:" Show 3-4 examples: "Adjacent 4-storey building south" → "Ground floor in shadow after 2pm - daylight-dependent spaces on upper floors." "Bus stop 50m east" → "Main entrance faces east to capture pedestrian flow." "Heavy traffic noise from north" → "Buffer zone on north edge, living spaces set back." Use a clean two-column layout with arrows between data and implication.

Slide 7 (SYNTHESIS): "Step 3: Write 3 site-driven design principles." Show a mockup of 3 numbered principles with blank lines, like a notebook page. Text below: "These 3 principles guide EVERY major design decision. If your concept doesn't connect to your site analysis, your lecturers will notice."

Slide 8 (DIAGRAM FORMAT): "Format your site analysis page like this:" Show a simple page layout mockup: LEFT half labeled "Raw data / diagram" and RIGHT half labeled "What it means for your design." Below: "NOT just: sun path + wind rose + done." Include a simple before/after comparison if possible.

Slide 9 (COMMON MISTAKES): "5 mistakes that make lecturers tune out:" 1. Sun path diagram with zero design implications. 2. Ignoring everything beyond your site boundary. 3. Site analysis that has no connection to your concept. 4. Only analyzing climate - ignoring social/historical context. 5. Copy-pasting the same analysis format for every project.

Slide 10 (CTA): "I made a free site analysis checklist with this exact framework." "It's the same method I used to win best thesis across engineering, science, and architecture." "Link in bio - it's free." Include @studwalldan handle and a simple arrow pointing up (to bio link). Background: #E86A1C burnt orange with white text for contrast.

DESIGN REQUIREMENTS:
- Reference this Figma template for layout feel: https://www.figma.com/community/file/1511824862327642199/modern-sale-facebook-portrait-ad
- Clean, minimal, editorial aesthetic - NOT corporate or agency-looking
- Font: Inter or similar clean sans-serif
- Primary accent color: #E86A1C (burnt orange) - use for headers, highlights, slide 10 background
- Body text: dark gray or black on white/off-white background
- Include simple SVG/CSS diagrams for slides 2, 4, 5, 6, 8 (site plans, arrows, zones, comparison layouts)
- Each slide should work as a standalone image AND flow as a sequence
- Should feel like a smart student sharing their notes, not a marketing agency selling something
- Swipe indicators on slides 1-9 (small arrow or "swipe →" text in corner)

Generate each slide as a self-contained HTML file (1080x1350px viewport), screenshot each one, and deliver all 10 images.
