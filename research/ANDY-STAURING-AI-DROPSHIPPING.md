## Ad Strategy: Fast Testing Protocol

### Campaign Structure
- **Day 1-2:** Single campaign, 3-4 video ad variations, $100/day budget
- **Day 3-4:** Realized videos underperforming, pivoted to **image ads**
- **Day 4:** New campaign with 3 image creatives, $75 → $150/day budget
- **Day 5-6:** Facebook approval issues killed momentum (ad got rejected mid-campaign)
- **Day 7:** Budget exhausted

### Testing Methodology
1. **Competitor analysis first:** Scraped competitor's Facebook Ad Library, identified top-performing formats
2. **Format > creative:** He didn't optimize copy/angle - he optimized **format** (image vs video)
3. **Kill losers fast:** If an ad spent $60-70 without a sale, turned it off same day
4. **Winner gets 100% budget:** Once image ad #1 got 3 purchases, he killed all other ads and scaled it
5. **No targeting:** Broad, open campaign. Let Meta find buyers.
6. **Duplicate winners when scaling:** When he increased budget $75 → $150, he duplicated the winning ad to avoid killing the learning phase

### Creative Testing Framework (APPLICABLE TO KONTEKS)
**Image ad format that won:**
- Product-focused hero image (necklace on clean background)
- Emotional headline overlay: "Keep them close always" / "Piece of your heart"
- Simple, benefit-driven copy
- **NOT UGC-style talking head videos** (those lost to static images)

**Why images beat videos:**
- Faster thumb-stop
- Lower production cost (he paid $90 for video ads, made images free in Canva)
- Easier to test variations (he created 3-4 image variants in minutes)

**Meta ads insight:** Even in 2026, **simple product images with emotional copy can outperform UGC videos** for impulse buys.

## What Worked

### ✅ Winning Ad Creative
- **Image ads over video ads** (complete flip from conventional wisdom)
- **Ad #1 (image):** Spent $89, got 7 purchases, 1.6-1.8 ROAS
- **Emotional angle:** "Keep them close always" (memorial/sentimental positioning)
- **Product-first format:** Clean product shot + text overlay

### ✅ Store Optimizations That Drove Conversions
1. **Variant upsells:** 1 pendant ($29.99) → 2 pendants ($49.95) → 3 pendants ($69.95)
   - Someone bought 2 pendants = $56.97 order (2x AOV)
2. **Engraving upsell:** $16.99 add-on (pure profit, no extra cost to fulfill)
   - Tested at $9.99 (more conversions) then back to $16.99 (more margin)
3. **Aggressive pricing:** Undercut competitor ($29.99 vs $39.99)
4. **Fast shipping:** Paid $1-2 extra for 5-8 day shipping (vs 7-15 day) to reduce refund risk

### ✅ Conversion Rate
- **3.65% site-wide conversion rate** (industry standard for cold traffic)
- **7 orders from $116 in tracked ad spend on Day 4** = proof of concept

## What Failed

### ❌ Video Ads (Initial Campaign)
- Spent $163 on Day 2, got 3 purchases (barely breakeven)
- Spent $116 on Day 3, got 1 purchase (deep red)
- **Killed entirely by Day 4** in favor of images

### ❌ Facebook Approval Issues (The Real Killer)
- **Day 5:** Winning ad got disapproved mid-campaign for "invalid product destination ID"
- Ad was OFF for 5+ hours while he troubleshot
- Lost all momentum - had to duplicate and restart learning phase
- **Cost him the challenge** - couldn't recover in time

### ❌ Budget Allocation
- Spent 80%+ of budget on ads before finding winner
- Should have tested cheaper traffic sources first (organic, influencers, email list)
- **Lesson:** Don't go all-in on cold Meta ads without validated offer

### ❌ Product Fulfillment Screwups
- Forgot to add "upload your pet photo" field on store for first 2 days
- Had to email customers to get photos after they'd already purchased
- Likely killed conversions (people added to cart but couldn't complete order)

## Frameworks for Education/Skool Marketing

### 1. **Fast Kill/Scale Protocol**
- If ad spends $60-70 without conversion → kill same day
- If ad gets 3+ conversions at <$30 CPA → scale budget +50% next day
- Don't wait for "statistical significance" - move fast

### 2. **Format Testing Before Creative Testing**
Andy didn't test 10 different angles - he tested **2 formats** (video vs image) and found images won.

**For Konteks Kourse:**
- Test **testimonial images** (screenshot of student win + quote) vs **course preview images** (Dan teaching in Figma screenshot)
- Test **static carousel** (3-5 images showing transformation) vs **single hero image**
- Test **text-heavy** (list of what's inside) vs **visual-heavy** (just Dan's face + logo)

### 3. **Competitor Swipe Intelligence**
Andy's entire strategy: **scrape Facebook Ad Library → identify winners → make variations → test fast**

**For Konteks:**
1. Search Ad Library for: "architecture course", "design education", "Skool community"
2. Find ads running 30+ days (= profitable)
3. Deconstruct: What format? What hook? What CTA?
4. Don't copy - **remix the format with your brand voice**

### 4. **Emotional Positioning > Logical Benefits**
Andy's winner: "Keep them close always" (emotion) NOT "Custom engraved pet necklace" (feature)

**For Konteks:**
- "Finally understand architecture like Dan does" (emotion: clarity, confidence)
- NOT "12-chapter course on architectural thinking" (logical, boring)

### 5. **AOV Optimization via Upsells**
Andy's AOV jumped from $29.99 → $56.97 when someone bought 2 pendants.

**For Konteks:**
- $29/mo membership (base)
- $99 one-time "Fundamentals Bundle" (Chapters 1-4 + bonus)
- $199 "Full Access" (all 12 chapters + 1:1 office hours)

Test upsells at checkout: "Add Fundamentals Bundle for $70 more (save $29)"

## Funnel Structure

### Store Flow
1. **Cold traffic from Facebook ad** → 2. **Product page** (variant selector, upsell, urgency)
   - Variant upsell: 1 pendant → 2 pendants → 3 pendants
   - Add-on upsell: Engraving ($16.99)
   - Urgency: "First 10 units at $29.99" (fake scarcity)
3. **Cart** → 4. **Checkout** (Shopify standard)
   - No email capture, no quiz, no pre-sell page
   - Pure impulse buy funnel

### **For Konteks (DON'T copy this funnel):**
Andy's funnel works for **$30 impulse buy**. It WON'T work for **$29/mo subscription**.

**Better funnel for Konteks:**
1. **Cold traffic** → 2. **Free lead magnet** (quiz, PDF, video training) to capture email
2. **Email nurture** (3-5 day sequence showing transformation)
3. **Sales page** with trial offer ("First month $1" or "7-day free trial")
4. **Checkout** → Skool membership

**Why:** $29/mo subscription requires more trust than $30 one-time necklace.

## Key Takeaways for Konteks Kourse

### ✅ DO
1. **Test image ads first** (cheaper, faster to iterate than video)
2. **Kill losers within 24-48 hours** (don't let bad ads bleed budget)
3. **Broad targeting** (let Meta find buyers, don't over-narrow)
4. **Steal competitor formats** (Ad Library is free market research)
5. **Optimize AOV with upsells** (offer "Fundamentals Bundle" at checkout)
6. **Price aggressively** (undercut competitors to win early traction)

### ❌ DON'T
1. **Don't overspend on video production** (Andy's $90 video ads lost to free Canva images)
2. **Don't assume video > static** (test both, data decides)
3. **Don't rely on "AI tools"** for creative (Andy didn't, despite the title)
4. **Don't ignore Facebook approval issues** (one rejection killed his entire campaign)
5. **Don't copy dropshipping funnels for subscription products** (different buyer psychology)

## Meta Algorithm Learnings (from Andy's experience)

1. **Meta rewards fast conversions:** The ad that got 3 purchases in first 24 hours got 100% of budget
2. **Learning phase is fragile:** When he duplicated the winning ad, it had to re-learn (lost momentum)
3. **Disapprovals kill campaigns:** Even temporary disapproval (5 hours) destroyed performance
4. **Budget increases need duplication:** Going from $75 → $150 budget, he duplicated the ad to preserve learning
5. **No targeting works:** He used ZERO targeting (no age, no interests) and got 3.65% CVR

**For Konteks:** Don't overthink targeting. Broad + fast testing > narrow + slow testing.
