## Key Takeaways for Ad Creative

### Dan's Voice Patterns
1. **Contrarian positioning:** "Students think X, but pros know Y"
2. **Slightly harsh truth-telling:** "overdesigned and under-thought"
3. **Simple frameworks:** 3-step rules, numbered lists
4. **Casual but confident:** "veeeeery quick lil" mixed with authoritative advice
5. **Anti-academic stance:** challenges traditional architecture school thinking

### What Works Best
- **Anti-academic angle outperforms how-to by 3x** (219K vs 71K views)
- **Contrarian hooks** ("Most students think... but pros know...") drive highest engagement
- **Simple numbered frameworks** (3-Render Rule) are shareable and memorable
- **Student pain points** (presentation anxiety, site analysis confusion) resonate

### Ad Angle Recommendations
1. **Lead with contrarian takes** - "Architecture school teaches you wrong" positioning
2. **Use Dan's harsh-but-helpful tone** - students respond to direct truth over coddling
3. **Frame solutions as simple rules** - "The 3-X Rule" format works
4. **Target presentation anxiety** - "stop overcomplicating your boards" messaging
5. **Position Konteks as anti-establishment** - "What they won't teach you in studio"

### Hashtag Strategy
Most effective tags from top videos:
- #ArchitectureStudent (broad reach)
- #DesignTips (how-to intent)
- #ArchitecturePresentation (specific pain point)
- #RenderLikeAPro (aspiration)
- #ArchitectureSchool (ICP targeting)

### Creative Testing Priority
1. **Test contrarian angles first** - they outperform how-to content
2. **Use Dan's face + text overlay** - personality-driven
3. **Keep videos under 75 seconds** - sweet spot is 60-70s
4. **Lead with the contrarian statement** - hook in first 3 seconds
5. **End with simple framework** - make it shareable
