# Consulting Offer Research: "The Architect Your Architect Hires"
**Subject:** Dan Fouché (@studwalldan) - Homeowner Architecture Consulting Pivot
**Date:** March 26, 2026
**Research Depth:** Market validation, competitor analysis, pricing, offer structures, ad strategy

---

## Executive Summary

**THE VERDICT: TRAP, NOT GAP.**

This is a pivot away from Dan's core strength (architecture education) into a crowded, commoditized service market where she has no positioning advantage. The demand exists, but the economics don't support cold Meta traffic acquisition for B2C consulting.

**Key Issues:**
- Dan's current audience (27.5K TikTok, 363 Skool members) are **students**, not homeowners
- Homeowner consulting is high-friction, high-touch, low-scalability
- Average architect consultation: $3,250 (Bark nationwide). Too expensive for impulse buy from cold ads.
- Interior design consultations (Havenly): $159-$499/room. More ad-friendly, but Dan isn't an interior designer.
- **No one is running Meta ads for architecture consultations** - that's a signal, not an opportunity.

---

## 1. MARKET VALIDATION

### Demand Signal: EXISTS (but fragmented and price-sensitive)

**Platforms Where Homeowners Seek Architecture Help:**
- **Bark:** Architecture services listed, nationwide avg $3,250
- **Houzz Pro:** 1,499+ architect listings, primarily full-service firms (not consultations)
- **Thumbtack:** Architects available, but "consultation" category redirects to general architect services
- **JustAnswer:** No architecture category (they have doctors, lawyers, vets, mechanics - but not architects)

**What This Tells Us:**
- Homeowners DO hire architects, but for **projects**, not consultations
- "Second opinion" services are rare - most hire one architect and stick with them
- The pain point isn't "I need a second opinion on my architect" - it's "I need an architect, period"

### Pain Points (Inferred from Platform Positioning)

Based on Bark, Houzz, and Havenly positioning:

1. **Budget Uncertainty** - "How much will this cost?" is the #1 question
2. **Decision Paralysis** - Too many choices, unclear what "good" looks like
3. **Scope Creep** - Projects expand beyond initial budget
4. **Communication Breakdown** - Architect speaks in jargon, homeowner feels lost
5. **Trust Issues** - "Is my architect ripping me off?" / "Are these changes necessary?"

**BUT:** These pain points are solved by **better architects**, not by hiring a second architect to check the first one.

## 2. COMPETITOR ANALYSIS

### The Three Scraped Competitors - None Are Doing This

**Home Design Academy (homedesignacademy.com)**
- **Audience:** Aspiring designers (not homeowners)
- **Offer:** "How to Start Designing Homes Professionally Without Going Back to School"
- **Revenue Model:** Course for designers ($5K-$30K per project claims)
- **Why They're NOT Doing Homeowner Consulting:** Because teaching designers to design is more scalable and higher-margin than consulting for homeowners.

**Rafii Nez (rafiinez.com/kitchencourse)**
- **Audience:** DIYers + design professionals
- **Offer:** Kitchen Design Course - $89 one-time, 160+ min video
- **Focus:** One room (kitchens only), self-serve course
- **Why They're NOT Doing Homeowner Consulting:** Because a $89 course scales infinitely. Consulting doesn't.

**Maria Ulashchenko (mariaulashchenko.de)**
- **Audience:** Architecture students/graduates looking for jobs
- **Offer:** Architecture portfolio guide, "Built from 200+ real reviews"
- **Positioning:** Academic/professional development
- **Why They're NOT Doing Homeowner Consulting:** Because students are a better audience - they're motivated, they have time, they're easier to convert.

### Why Isn't Anyone Doing Homeowner Architecture Consulting?

**Theory 1: It's a Graveyard (Most Likely)**
- High customer acquisition cost (CAC) - homeowners need education before they value a "second opinion"
- Low lifetime value (LTV) - most people renovate once every 5-10 years
- High support burden - every consultation is custom, not productized
- Liability risk - if you give advice and something goes wrong, you're liable

**Theory 2: It's a Gap (Unlikely)**
- Maybe no one has productized it well enough
- Maybe there's a way to do it at scale via video/async
- Maybe the positioning is wrong ("second opinion" vs "design review")

**Verdict:** If this were a real gap, Havenly or Modsy would have added it. They haven't. That's telling.

## 3. PRICING RESEARCH

### What Architects Actually Charge

**Full Architectural Services (Bark Data):**
- Nationwide Average: **$3,250**
- Pacific Region: **$5,000**
- South Atlantic: **$5,000**
- West South Central: **$2,400**

**What This Includes:**
- Design advice
- On-site survey
- Professional CAD drawings
- Drawing amendments
- Planning applications
- Planning revisions

**Typical Billing Models:**
1. **Percentage of Construction Cost:** 8-15% (standard)
2. **Hourly:** $100-$250/hr (consultation rates)
3. **Fixed Fee:** $3K-$10K+ (depends on scope)

### Comparable Services (Interior Design)

**Havenly (Online Interior Design):**
- **Online Design:** $159/room (was $199)
- **In-Person Design:** $499/room for first room (was $699)
- **What You Get:** Style quiz, designer match, 3D rendering, product sourcing, exclusive discounts
- **Positioning:** "Designing your dream home just became a reality"

**Key Takeaway:** Havenly's $159/room is ad-friendly. It's low enough for impulse buy, high enough to be valuable. But that's **interior design**, not architecture.

### Proposed Pricing for Dan's Service

**Problem:** Architecture consulting doesn't fit neatly into Havenly's model.

| Service Type | Price Range | Scalability | Ad-Friendliness |
|-------------|-------------|-------------|-----------------|
| Hourly Consultation | $150-$300/hr | Low | Poor (unclear value) |
| One-Time Design Review | $500-$1,500 | Medium | Medium (needs education) |
| DIY Course | $89-$299 | High | High (clear outcome) |
| Monthly Membership | $49-$99/mo | High | Medium (needs retention) |
| Productized Package | $299-$799 | Medium | High (if positioned right) |

**Sweet Spot for Cold Meta Ads:** $299-$499 for a fixed-scope design review package.

**But:** That's still 2-3x more expensive than Havenly, and Dan's target audience doesn't know they need it yet.

**Pros:** High-value, clear ROI, productized enough to scale
**Cons:** Liability risk, requires professional insurance, hard to sell cold

## 6. HONEST ASSESSMENT

### Is This a Good Idea or a Trap?

**TRAP.**

Here's why:

### 1. **Audience Mismatch**
- Dan's current audience: 27.5K TikTok followers (architecture students)
- Skool community: 363 members at $29/mo (students, not homeowners)
- **To pivot to homeowners, Dan needs to build a NEW audience from scratch.**
- That means starting over on content, SEO, and paid ads.
- **Why abandon a working flywheel?**

### 2. **Unit Economics Don't Work**
- **CAC for homeowner consulting:** $200-$500 (cold traffic, high friction)
- **Average Order Value:** $500-$1,500 (one-time purchase)
- **LTV:** Same as AOV (no repeat purchase)
- **Gross Margin:** 80% (after platform fees, tooling)
- **Net Margin:** 50% (after Dan's time)
- **Break-even:** Need 3-5 months of ad spend to ROI
- **Risk:** High churn if service doesn't deliver instant ROI

Compare that to Dan's current model:
- **CAC for students:** $50-$150
- **AOV:** $29/mo x 12 months = $348/year
- **LTV:** $500-$3,000 (multi-year retention + upsells)
- **Gross Margin:** 95% (digital product)
- **Net Margin:** 85% (minimal delivery cost)

**The student model is 5x better than homeowner consulting.**

### 3. **Operational Complexity**
- Every homeowner consultation is **custom** - different house, different architect, different plans
- Can't templatize like a course
- High support burden (emails, calls, follow-ups)
- Liability risk (if Dan's advice is wrong, she could be sued)
- Requires professional liability insurance ($2K-$5K/year)

### 4. **Market Timing**
- Homeowners only care about this when they're **actively renovating**
- That's a 6-12 month window every 5-10 years
- Outside that window, they don't care
- **Compare that to students:** Always learning, always engaged

### 5. **Positioning Problem**
- "The architect your architect hires" is clever, but confusing
- Homeowners don't think "I need a second architect"
- They think "I need a better architect" or "I need to trust my architect"
- **Positioning Dan as "the fixer" undermines the entire architecture profession**
- That might alienate her core audience (architects and students)

### What Would Need to Be True for This to Work?

1. **Dan would need to shift ALL content to homeowners** (abandon students)
2. **She'd need to run $10K+/mo in ads for 6-12 months** to validate demand
3. **She'd need professional liability insurance** (and accept legal risk)
4. **She'd need to productize the service** (templates, checklists, automation)
5. **She'd need a guarantee** ("Find $5K in savings or don't pay")
6. **She'd need to accept 50%+ lower margins** than her current business

**Even if all that were true, the student business is STILL better.**
