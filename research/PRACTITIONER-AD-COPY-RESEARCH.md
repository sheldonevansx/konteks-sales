## SOURCE 1: Reddit r/FacebookAds - 10,000 Video Ad Analysis

**Source:** Analysis of 10,000+ top-performing video ads across beauty, supplements, food & beverage, apps, SaaS  
**Key Insight:** "The ads that perform best usually start with the same types of hooks. Not the exact same words. But the same patterns."

### 19 PROVEN HOOK TYPES (First 3 Seconds)

1. **Apology Hook**
   - Pattern: Starts with an apology
   - Why it works: Creates immediate tension, makes people want to know what happened
   - Examples: "I'm sorry for not sharing this sooner..." / "I have to apologize..."

2. **Expert Angle**
   - Pattern: States relevant expertise or credentials
   - Why it works: Establishes authority immediately
   - Examples: "As a registered dietitian..." / "I've been a foot surgeon for 20 years..."

3. **Audience Callout**
   - Pattern: Calls out a specific group directly
   - Why it works: People notice when directly addressed
   - Examples: "If you're a working mom..." / "Carnivore dieters, this is for you..."

4. **Bold Claim**
   - Pattern: Makes a strong statement
   - Why it works: Creates tension, people want to test if it's true
   - Examples: "This is the only supplement you actually need..." / "We invented a new way to sleep..."

5. **Did You Know**
   - Pattern: Suggests knowledge gap
   - Why it works: Curiosity trigger
   - Examples: "Did you know your tap water might be..." / "Most people don't know..."

6. **FOMO (Fear of Missing Out)**
   - Pattern: Suggests viewer is missing something
   - Why it works: Strong attention trigger, motivated to avoid being left behind
   - Examples: "Everyone's switching to..." / "If you haven't tried..."

7. **Went Viral**
   - Pattern: Says product went viral on TikTok/IG
   - Why it works: Popularity = social proof
   - Examples: "This went viral on TikTok for a reason..." / "10M views later..."

8. **How I Did It**
   - Pattern: Promises method behind result
   - Why it works: Combines proof + curiosity
   - Examples: "How I cleared my skin in 30 days..." / "The method I used to..."

9. **Founder Intro**
   - Pattern: Founder introducing themselves
   - Why it works: Real person = trust + human connection
   - Examples: "Hi, I'm Karen, founder of..." / "We started this company because..."

10. **I Wish I Knew**
    - Pattern: Shares lesson with hindsight
    - Why it works: Signals missed value, want to learn now
    - Examples: "I wish I knew this before..." / "If only someone told me..."

11. **Knowledge Drop**
    - Pattern: Shares quick insight
    - Why it works: Immediate value in first seconds
    - Examples: "Here's what your body actually needs..." / "The science says..."

12. **Let's Compare**
    - Pattern: Puts two options side by side
    - Why it works: Creates decision frame
    - Examples: "Traditional deodorant vs. natural..." / "Before vs. After..."

13. **Debunk**
    - Pattern: Challenges common belief
    - Why it works: When beliefs are challenged, people want to hear other side
    - Examples: "Everything you know about X is wrong..." / "This myth is costing you..."

14. **Statistics**
    - Pattern: Uses specific number/stat
    - Why it works: Numbers feel concrete and credible
    - Examples: "80% of women experience..." / "After testing 47 different..."

15. **Packing Orders**
    - Pattern: Shows real orders being packed
    - Why it works: Visible proof of active buying = real demand
    - Examples: B-roll of warehouse, shipping labels, products being packed

16. **Reply to Comment**
    - Pattern: Responds to specific question
    - Why it works: Native to platform, conversational, lowers ad resistance
    - Examples: "Someone asked in the comments..." / "Great question..."

17. **X Reasons**
    - Pattern: Promises reasons behind choice/claim
    - Why it works: Logic behind conclusion, stay for justification
    - Examples: "3 reasons I switched to..." / "Here's why 10,000 customers..."

18. **This Is...**
    - Pattern: Introduces specific person via narrator
    - Why it works: Specific people feel real
    - Examples: "This is Sarah. She struggled with..." / "Meet John..."

19. **Study-Based**
    - Pattern: Uses study, trial, data-backed finding
    - Why it works: Study reference = trust
    - Examples: "A Stanford study found..." / "Research shows..."

## SOURCE 2: Ecom Playbook - Practitioner Frameworks

**Source:** Analysis of Davie Fogarty (The Oodie, $850M brand), Dara Denney (Thesis Agency creative strategist), Nick Theriot (Meta ads specialist), Alex Fedotoff (scaled $179k→$600k/mo), Jordan Welch (multiple 7-fig businesses), Sebastian Esqueda (TikTok brand builder)

### DARA DENNEY - UGC Script Framework (AIDA)

**AIDA = Attention, Interest, Desire, Action**

1. **Hook** (First 3 seconds)
   - Grab attention
   - Use one of the 19 hook types above

2. **Problem Agitator**
   - Describe common problem
   - Agitate the pain
   - Make it specific and relatable

3. **Product Intro**
   - Position as solution to problem
   - Name the mechanism if possible

4. **Product How-To** (if complex)
   - Show how it works
   - Demo/unboxing

5. **Benefits/Testimonials**
   - Paint better life after product
   - Social proof

6. **CTA (Call to Action)**
   - Catchy, clear
   - Action verb + product name

### ALEX FEDOTOFF - 2026 Meta Ads Strategy

**OLD WAY (DOESN'T WORK):**
- ❌ Interest targeting
- ❌ Lookalike audiences
- ❌ Horizontal scaling

**NEW WAY (Andromeda Update):**
- ✅ Creative diversification is THE scaling method
- ✅ Consolidated campaigns (less campaigns, more spend per campaign)
- ✅ Broad targeting only
- ✅ Facebook = "creative-led targeting platform"

**Key Insight:**
- FB scans your creative and shows to matching demographics
- Using old people in ads? FB shows to old people
- **NEVER use young models to sell to older demos** (common mistake)

**Creative Inventory Needed:**
- Statics
- UGC
- Podcast format
- Demo/Unboxing
- Testimonials
- Street interviews
- Different angles for different market segments

### NICK THERIOT - Daily Scaling SOP

**Campaign Structure:**
- ONE CBO campaign per business objective
- Don't create new campaigns to scale - adjust budget on existing

**Daily Decision Tree:**
1. Did we hit KPI yesterday?
   - YES → Increase spend 20%
   - NO → Wait 4 days for rebalance before adjusting
2. Never panic-drop budget
3. All adjustments = 20% increments

**Hard Deck:**
- Minimum daily spend you never go below
- Maintains testing volume
- Only increase from hard deck when KPIs return

### DARA DENNEY - Creative Metrics

**Key Metrics to Track:**
- **Hook rate:** 3-sec video views / impressions
- **Hold rate:** thruplays / impressions
- **CTR:** unique outbound clicks / impressions
- Breakdown by: age, gender, placements (FB vs IG vs Stories vs Reels)

**Creative Strategy Process:**
- 4 creative concepts per month (weekly deliverables)
- Each ticket = 3-6 assets testing ONE variable (usually hook)
- UGC creators/studio = 4-6 weeks lead time

## SOURCE 3: Conversion Copywriting Principles

### HOPKINS ADVERTISING PRINCIPLES
- **Specificity sells:** "Lost 14 pounds" > "Lost weight"
- **Reason-why copy:** Always give a reason
- **Telegram style:** Short, punchy sentences
- **Test everything:** Headlines, offers, angles

### SCHWARTZ AWARENESS LEVELS (Stage 1-5)

**Unaware:** Doesn't know they have a problem → Lead with content  
**Problem-aware:** Knows problem, not solutions → Lead with pain  
**Solution-aware:** Knows solutions exist, not yours → Lead with mechanism  
**Product-aware:** Knows your product, hasn't bought → Lead with proof  
**Most aware:** Ready to buy → Lead with price and CTA

**For landing pages from ads:** Assume **problem-aware to solution-aware**

### PAS (Pain-Agitate-Solve)

**Pain:** Name exact situation in their language  
**Agitate:** Show consequence of doing nothing  
**Solution:** Present product as obvious answer

### HORMOZI VALUE EQUATION

```
Value = (Dream Outcome × Perceived Likelihood) / (Time Delay × Effort)
```

Copy must:
- Maximize top (big outcome, high believability)
- Minimize bottom (fast results, low effort)

## EMAIL COPYWRITING (Plain Text Conversion)

### WALSHYMAILS METHODOLOGY (Inferred from Practitioner Consensus)

**What Converts:**
- Plain text emails (no fancy design)
- Conversational tone
- Short paragraphs
- One idea per email
- Clear CTA

**Subject Line Formulas:**
- Curiosity gap: "I noticed something weird..."
- Direct benefit: "How to X without Y"
- Personal: "Quick question about..."
- Urgency: "Closing in 3 hours"
- Controversy: "Unpopular opinion:"

**Body Structure:**
- Hook (first line stops the scan)
- Story or insight (2-3 short paragraphs)
- Transition to product (natural, not salesy)
- CTA (one link, clear action)
- Signature

**Avoid:**
- Promotional language
- Multiple CTAs
- Corporate tone
- Heavy formatting
- Long paragraphs (>4 lines)

## TESTING METHODOLOGY

### DARA DENNEY - Theme-Based Testing

**Process:**
1. Create 4 creative concepts per month
2. Each concept tests ONE variable (usually hook)
3. 3-6 assets per concept
4. Monitor daily, kill/scale at 3-7 days
5. Monthly creative meetings: review winners/losers

**What to Test:**
- Hook variations (biggest impact)
- UGC vs branded creative
- Different market segments (working moms vs. health conditions)
- Platform-specific formats (Reels vs. Stories vs. Feed)

### ALEX FEDOTOFF - 3-Hour Surf Scaling

**Method:**
- Check campaigns every 3 hours (9am, 12pm, 3pm, 6pm, 12am)
- Ramp profitable campaigns (2x budget)
- Shut down underperformers
- Reset all to base budget at midnight
- Can spend 2-4x more profitably vs. static budgets

## CREATIVE STRATEGY (STATIC IMAGE ADS)

### Layout Best Practices:
- **Visual hierarchy:** Main image/product top, text overlay mid, CTA bottom
- **Text placement:** Don't cover product, use high-contrast colors
- **Image quality:** UGC-style often outperforms studio shots
- **Text overlay:** Max 20% of image (FB guideline)

### What Makes Ads Scroll-Stopping:
- **Pattern interrupt:** Unexpected visual or statement
- **Specificity:** Concrete details, not vague claims
- **Human faces:** Direct eye contact with camera
- **Before/After:** Side-by-side comparisons
- **Social proof:** Real testimonials, user-generated content

### UGC vs Branded Creative Performance:
- **UGC (User-Generated Content):**
  - Feels native to platform
  - Lower ad resistance
  - Higher trust
  - Cheaper to produce
  - Works best for: cold traffic, awareness, testing

- **Branded Creative:**
  - More polished
  - Better for: retargeting, brand building, premium products
  - Higher production cost
  - Can feel "salesy" if not done right

## RETARGETING CREATIVE DIFFERENTIATION

**Cold Traffic (Awareness):**
- Broad appeal
- Problem-focused hooks
- Social proof (viral, popular)
- Educational angle

**Warm Traffic (Consideration):**
- Mechanism-focused
- How it works
- Testimonials
- Comparison to alternatives

**Hot Traffic (Retargeting):**
- Urgency/Scarcity
- Objection handling
- Limited-time offers
- Cart abandonment reminders
- Different angle than original ad (don't repeat same message)

## WORDS/PHRASES TO AVOID (AI Slop)

**From Humanizer Skill:**
- No em-dashes (use hyphens)
- No "crucial/delve/landscape/pivotal/showcase/tapestry/vibrant"
- No negative parallelisms ("It's not X, it's Y")
- No rule of three ("Fast. Simple. Powerful.")
- No promotional superlatives ("revolutionary, game-changing, unlock, unleash")

**From Conversion Copywriter:**
- No "in today's" anything
- No "leverage/utilize" (use "use")
- No "comprehensive" (say what it actually covers)
- No bro energy ("STOP LOSING MONEY!")
- No vague superlatives ("ultimate, best, world-class")

## EDUCATION/COURSE PRODUCT ADAPTATIONS

### Hook Adaptations for Courses:
1. **Apology:** "I'm sorry I didn't share this framework sooner with architecture students..."
2. **Expert Angle:** "After teaching 500+ students, I noticed a pattern..."
3. **Audience Callout:** "Architecture students, if you're drowning in deadlines..."
4. **Bold Claim:** "Most architecture courses teach backwards. Here's why..."
5. **Did You Know:** "Did you know 80% of students fail because of one missing framework?"
6. **FOMO:** "While other students are pulling all-nighters, these students finish in half the time..."
7. **How I Did It:** "How I went from failing student to top of my class in one semester..."
8. **Debunk:** "Everything you learned about design briefs is wrong..."
9. **Statistics:** "87% of architecture students struggle with X. Here's the fix..."

### Copy Adjustments:
- Replace "product" with "course/framework/system"
- Focus on transformation: "before struggling student" → "after confident designer"
- Use academic pain points: deadlines, all-nighters, harsh critiques, unclear briefs
- Social proof: student testimonials, grade improvements, portfolio wins
- Mechanism: named methodology ("The Konteks Method")

## KEY TAKEAWAYS

1. **Hooks are formulaic** - Use the 19 proven patterns, adapt to your product
2. **Broad targeting + creative diversity** = 2026 Meta strategy
3. **Test systematically** - One variable at a time, theme-based
4. **UGC outperforms** for cold traffic
5. **Plain text emails** convert better than designed emails
6. **Specificity always wins** - Concrete details > vague claims
7. **Platform scans creative** - Match demographics in ad to target audience
8. **Don't panic-scale** - Wait 4 days for rebalance
9. **Creative volume is scaling** - Not targeting tricks
10. **Attribution tools mandatory** - Platform ROAS ≠ actual ROAS

## Jason Wojciechowicz (@thejasonwojo) - Organic + Paid Flywheel Strategy

**Source:** Instagram Reel (Apr 2, 2026) - 113K views, 6.8K likes
**Link:** https://www.instagram.com/reel/DWpYpZiCeU8/

### Full Transcript

"These are my stats for the last 30 days, posting content, going really hard. If you want to build more trust and you want to reduce your sales cycles, you've got to just be more consistent. I usually get about 15 million views a month, and I get 21 million views. I've got an extra 1.5 million interactions, 6,000 new followers that I can now DM and outreach which I have a setting team who does that, and it's just from consistent, consistent content. The biggest bridge between where you're at and where you want to go is using organic to find your winning ads, and that's what I've been doing, right? And also doing a content ad-hook strategy where I punch a bunch of videos out at $2 a day on ads, and I'm just getting three second video plays, and then I retarget all of those three second video plays to testimony videos, and those testimony videos are retargeting all the three second video plays, and then I'm grabbing all that and running conversion-based ads back to my bottom of Funnel Ads, which is book a call and buy-down."

### Strategy Breakdown

**The Organic-to-Paid Flywheel:**

1. **Consistent organic content** (high volume) - builds trust, reduces sales cycles
   - His stats: 21M views/month, 1.5M interactions, 6K new followers/month
   - Has a setting team DMing new followers (outbound on inbound leads)

2. **Content Ad-Hook Strategy** (the key insight):
   - Takes organic content/hooks and runs them as ads at **$2/day per video**
   - Objective: NOT conversions. Just **3-second video plays** (cheapest objective)
   - This is pure audience building at minimal cost

3. **Retarget 3-sec viewers with testimonial videos**
   - Everyone who watched 3+ seconds of the hook videos gets retargeted
   - Retarget creative = customer testimonials/social proof
   - This moves them from "aware" to "trusting"

4. **Bottom-of-funnel conversion ads**
   - Retarget the testimonial viewers with conversion-optimized ads
   - CTA: "Book a call" or "Buy now"
   - This is where the actual ROAS happens

### Funnel Architecture

```
COLD (Organic + $2/day hook ads)
    → 3-sec video plays (custom audience)
        → WARM (Testimonial retarget ads)
            → 3-sec video plays on testimonials (custom audience)
                → HOT (Conversion ads - book a call / buy)
```

### Key Metrics
- 15-21M organic views/month
- 6,000 new followers/month from organic alone
- $2/day per hook video (testing budget)
- Uses 3-sec video view audiences as retarget pools

### Application to Konteks

**What Dan could do:**
1. Post Dan's talking head content consistently on IG/TikTok (she already does some)
2. Take the best-performing organic posts and boost them at $2/day for 3-sec views
3. Build a 3-sec video viewer audience in Meta
4. Retarget that audience with student testimonial content
5. Retarget testimonial viewers with the free guide (lead magnet) or Kourse offer

**Key difference from current approach:**
- Current: Cold traffic → lead magnet landing page (one step)
- Wojo's method: Cold hook → warm with social proof → convert (three steps)
- His method costs more in total ad spend but conversion rates are higher because trust is built before the ask

**Budget implication at $35/day:**
- Could split: $10/day on 5 hook videos at $2 each + $25/day on retarget/conversion
- But this requires Dan to have testimonial video content ready
- And it requires enough organic content volume to find "winning hooks"

### Source Assessment
- **Credibility:** PRACTITIONER - shows real dashboard stats (21M views verifiable via profile)
- **Novelty:** The $2/day hook testing is genuinely useful - it's creative testing at near-zero cost
- **Limitation:** His scale (15M+ views/month) makes the organic flywheel work. At Dan's current follower count, the organic side won't generate enough volume alone. The paid hook testing at $2/day is the applicable part.
