### 9. The "least likely to be faked" proof hierarchy

**Quote:** "You want to create the most compelling proof as possible, which is the type of proof that would be the least likely to be faked. **Context:** Explaining why live, specific, recent, similar-to-you proof is more persuasive.

**Tag:** [NEW - proof credibility hierarchy not explicitly in our skills]

**Analysis:** This is a genuinely useful framework for evaluating social proof quality. Our skills cover testimonials and social proof, but don't provide a ranked hierarchy of credibility factors.

**Framework (extracted):**
- **Weakest proof:** Faceless, anonymous, outdated, text-only, vague
- **Strongest proof:** In-person, live, specific to audience, recent, detailed results, named person

**Credibility factors (ranked):**
1. **Medium:** In-person > video > audio > image > text
2. **Timing:** Live > recent recorded > old recorded
3. **Specificity:** Looks like me > different from me
4. **Results:** Specific (3M to 30M) > vague (successful)
5. **Identity:** Named + photo > anonymous
6. **Recency:** This month > last year > 10 years ago

**Recommendation:** Add to conversion-copy skill under "Social Proof Hierarchy." Use Hormozi's framework to rank proof types.

**Konteks Application:**

### 12. Beauty/DTC scaling: "Top 10% of ads, slice the first 3 seconds"

**Quote:** "Look at the top 10% or 5% of ads, look at the first 3 seconds of each of those ads and see if you can model those three seconds with all of the remaining ads that you have. You can even slice that first three seconds off of the best winners and paste it on to another 100 plus ads."

**Context:** Scaling strategy for $2M beauty brand running 1,000+ ad variations.

**Tag:** [NEW - creative production efficiency hack not in our skills]

**Analysis:** This is a genuinely useful tactical insight for high-volume ad testing (100+ ads/month). Our skills cover hooks and creative strategy, but don't cover this specific "hook slicing" production technique.

**Framework:**
- Test 100+ ads
- Identify top 10% performers
- Isolate the first 3 seconds (the hook)
- Reuse that hook on 10-50 new ad variants (new body copy, new CTA, new visuals after the hook)
- Result: 10-50x more ads per winning hook

**Recommendation:** Add to ad-creative-formats skill under "Creative Production at Scale."

**Konteks Application:**

**"Hook Slicing for Production at Scale" (under Creative Production section)**
```
For high-volume ad testing (100+ ads/month):

Process:
1. Run 100+ ad variants
2. Identify top 10% performers (10 ads)
3. Isolate the first 3 seconds (the hook) from each winning ad
4. Extract the hook as a standalone clip
5. Create 10-50 new ad variants by pairing the winning hook with:
   - New body copy (after the hook)
   - New CTA
   - New end screen
   - New music/sound
6. Result: 10-50x content multiplication per winning hook

Example:
- Winning ad: "Architecture students - stop using Pinterest as your design process..."
- Extract first 3 seconds
- Pair with 20 different bodies:
  - Body 1: "You're copying aesthetics, hoping professors like it. Here's a better way..."
  - Body 2: "That's why you have no answer when they ask 'Why?' Here's what top students do..."
  - Body 3: "Most students waste 40 hours per project. This framework cuts it to 15..."

Why this works:
- The hook (first 3 seconds) = 80% of ad performance
- The body/CTA = 20% of ad performance
- Reusing winning hooks lets you test 50+ bodies without refilming hooks
- Production efficiency: 10x more ads with same effort

When to use:
- Ad spend >$10K/month
- Testing volume >50 ads/month
- Have 5+ winning hooks validated by data

When NOT to use:
- Low ad spend (<$5K/month) - not worth the complexity
- Low testing volume (<20 ads/month) - focus on fewer, better ads

Konteks Application:
- Current: Low volume, not applicable yet.
- Future: When scaling to $50K+/month ad spend, implement hook slicing.
```
